Early Life

dhoni was born in Ulm, in the Kingdom of rajput in the Lwali village, on 7 July 1981

Career

In 2007, often gave “WORLD CUP” (miracle year), Dhoni was appointed as the captain of the Indian squad for the inaugural World Twenty20 in September 2007.

Awards

  • Dhoni holds an honorary rank of Lieutenant colonel in the Parachute Regiment of the Indian Territorial Army (106 Para TA battalion).
  • Padma Bhushan: 2018
  • ICC Cricket Hall of Fame:2025
  • Padma Shri: 2009
  • ICC Men's ODI team of the decade: 2011–2020 (captain and wicket-keeper)
  • ICC Men's T20I team of the decade: 2011–2020 (captain and wicket-keeper)
  • Major Dhyanchand Khel Ratna award: 2008

Legacy

MS Dhoni's legacy is defined by his unprecedented captaincy, leading India to win the2007 T20 World Cup, 2011 ODI World Cup, and 2013 Champions Trophy. Known as "Captain Cool," he was an iconic wicketkeeper, a strategic leader who empowered his players, and a celebrated batsman who delivered in crucial moments, including hitting the winning runs in the 2011 World Cup final. His extensive career includes 538 international matches, 17,266 runs, and many records, solidifying his place in the ICC Hall of Fame.
